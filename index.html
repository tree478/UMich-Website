University of Michigan

The University of Michigan, founded in 1817, is the oldest in Michigan. It is a public research university located in Ann Arbor, Michigan. UMich is one of America's earliest research universities and was a founding member of the Association of American Universities. Being a highly prestigious university, it is ranked second best in research and has consistently ranked among the top universities in the US.

Why is UMich the Best College To Go To?

If you want to go to an Ivy League college but don’t have the money or don’t think you will get in, the University of Michigan is the perfect university for you! It is cheaper than Ivy League colleges and has a higher acceptance rate, but it is just as great. It has an excellent athletic program if you are an athlete. If you're looking to go into medicine, engineering, or software, UMich has extraordinary programs for that too! It also has a very diverse student body, meaning that you will never have to worry about not finding people with similar interests and backgrounds! UMich is also one of the wealthier schools in America, which not only means you get bragging rights, but if you are a research major, this also means there are no financial limits to what you can research!

The academic aspect of UMich is incredible and opens a whole new world of possibilities for what you can do in the future! What's more important is the lifestyle. UMich takes great pride in its campus life and makes sure that everyone has access to everything they need to be physically, emotionally, and mentally healthy. There are multiple socializing areas around the campus where you can chill and hang out with friends. Additionally, Michigan Dining provides a large variety of food for your diet, providing students with on-campus cafes, markets, and dining halls. And even better, UMich started a program called the Maiz and Blue Cupboard, through which you can get an MCard, which makes everything related to food free to buy!

UMich also goes to strenuous efforts to provide its students with good housing options. The campus contains 18 residence halls with 1480 apartments that can accommodate undergrads, grads, and students with families. For those of you looking for off-campus housing options, there is a Beyond the Diagram program, where you can find resources and student ambassadors who can connect you with different options.

Housing/food

  • You can go the the Michigan Unions(Michigan League, Michigan Union, and Pierpont Commons), which are like the “living rooms” of the campus, where you can chill, eat, and hang out with friends
  • Michigan Dining has twelve on-campus cafes, seven markets, and seven dining halls that all serve delicious, healthy, gourmet food options, so you never have to worry about being hungry!
  • The on-campus dorms, Michigan Housing, offers not only dorms, but recreational areas and learning programs so you will never have to worry about being bored
    • 18 residence halls
    • 1480 apartments
    • Can accommodate undergrads, grads, and students with families
  • They also have a Beyond the Diag program if you are looking for housing off-campus. You can find resources and there are student ambassadors who can connect you with off-campus housing options
  • Another really cool program is the Maize and Blue Cupboard, which is in place to make sure that everyone at UMich has access to healthy and nutritious food. You can get an MCard, through which, everything related to food is free! This means you can get free ingredients, kitchen and cookware, and even household supplies!
